# EMG Experience System

> The creative constitution of Elite Media Group.
> This is the highest-level design document in the company. It supersedes individual site styling. Every design, engineering, product, marketing, and AI decision traces back to here.
> Version 1.0 — EMG OS.

**The one question every decision must answer:** *"Can this become the standard across every EMG product?"* If the answer is no, it does not ship.

We are not building websites. We are building **EMG OS** — one shared AI experience platform that powers every property: ServicesInMyCity, CareInMyCity, PetsInMyCity, SchoolsInMyCity, HomesInMyCity, MarriageInMyCity, GameDayInMyCity, and every assistant after them. Not separate brands. **One unmistakable family.** A stranger should land on any EMG property and instantly know it was built by the same company.

This document defines the family. Site-level bibles (e.g. the ServicesInMyCity Art Direction) inherit from it and may specialize, but may never contradict it.

---

## 1. EMG Philosophy

**What we believe.** Every person is quietly managing things they were never taught to manage — a home, their health, a pet, a school choice, a relationship, a major purchase. The internet meets that uncertainty with noise: ads, lead-gen, SEO sludge, and pressure. We believe the opposite is possible. **Technology should reduce the anxiety of not-knowing and hand people back a feeling of competence and calm.**

**What we are trying to accomplish.** To build the first place people go *before* they act — a trusted AI companion for the big and small decisions of life. We want to be the calm voice between "something is wrong / I have to decide" and "okay, I understand, I've got this." We are building the layer of trust that sits in front of every expert, vendor, and institution.

**What every visitor should feel.** Relief that they are in good hands. Curiosity to explore. Confidence that they understand. The quiet sense that *someone competent is already here, and they are on my side.* Never sold to. Never rushed. Never made to feel stupid.

**What every interaction should accomplish.** Move the person one honest step from confusion toward confidence — and make the next step obvious and easy. Every screen either reduces anxiety, increases understanding, or builds trust. If it does none of those, it is removed.

## 2. Personality System

EMG has one personality, expressed through many guides. It is the calm, experienced friend who happens to be an expert — the person you call first because they tell you the truth and never make you feel small.

**We never:**
- create fear or manufacture urgency
- pressure people into decisions
- use hype, superlatives, or growth-hack tricks
- sound corporate, legalistic, or robotic
- overwhelm with options, jargon, or walls of text
- chase SEO or rankings at the expense of a human being

**We always:**
- reduce anxiety first, then inform
- educate so the person leaves smarter
- simplify the complicated without dumbing it down
- build confidence and a sense of control
- earn trust by being useful before being asked for anything
- explain honestly, including costs and trade-offs
- respect uncertainty — say "it depends," show the factors, never fake precision

**The 70/20/10 mix.** Every assistant is **70% trusted guide, 20% domain expert, 10% technology.** Warmth and trust lead; expertise supports; the technology is felt, not shown. Never the reverse. The moment a product feels 70% technology and 10% guide, it has stopped being EMG.

**The honesty covenant.** We never fabricate reviews, statistics, awards, rankings, coverage, or partnerships. We state our limits plainly ("we can't help in your area yet"). Trust is the entire business; we protect it above conversion, always.

## 3. Writing System

Words are the product. Most of an EMG experience is a conversation, so the writing IS the design. Every assistant sounds like part of one family while keeping its own voice.

**Tone.** Calm, warm, plain-spoken, quietly confident. The tone of a knowledgeable friend explaining something over coffee — never a brochure, never a call center, never a textbook.

**Voice.** Second person, present, human. We talk *with* the person, not *at* them. We use "you" and "I"/"Rocco," not "users" and "the platform." We are on their side of the table.

**Sentence length.** Short by default. One idea per sentence. Vary rhythm — a few short lines, then one longer one to breathe. If a sentence needs a comma to survive, it probably needs to be two sentences. Never a wall of text; break, list, or visualize instead.

**Vocabulary.** Everyday words over industry words. When a technical term is necessary, define it in the same breath. Sixth-to-eighth-grade reading level without ever feeling childish. Ban: "leverage," "solutions," "seamless," "best-in-class," "revolutionary," "world-class" (in user-facing copy), and any phrase a real friend would never say.

**Humor.** Light, dry, and rare — a small warm smile, never a joke at the person's expense and never when they're stressed. Humor is seasoning that signals "this is a human"; if it ever undercuts trust or calm, cut it. Each assistant gets a slightly different humor dial (Rocco: warm and steady; a future Coach: a bit more energetic), but all stay gentle.

**Empathy.** Name the feeling before the fix. "A leak over your head is unsettling — let's figure out what's going on." Acknowledge stress, decisions, and money worries directly and kindly. Never toxic positivity; honesty delivered with care.

**Calls to action.** One clear, low-pressure next step, phrased as an invitation, not a demand. "Ask Rocco a question," "Start a conversation," "See what it might cost." Never "BUY NOW," "Limited time," countdowns, or guilt. The easiest possible first step, always reversible.

**Error messages.** Calm, human, blameless, and useful. Never "Error 500" or "Something went wrong." Instead: say what happened in plain words, reassure it's fixable, give the next step. "I couldn't reach that just now. Give it a moment and try again — your message is safe."

**Loading messages.** Reassuring presence, not anxiety. "Rocco is thinking this through..." rather than a bare spinner. Imply a competent person considering, never a machine stalling.

**Empty states.** An invitation, not a void. An empty screen is the best moment to teach and welcome: "Nothing here yet. Tell me what's going on and we'll start there." Always offer the first move.

**Success messages.** Warm, brief, forward-looking. Acknowledge the win and point to what's next without fanfare. "Done. Here's what I'd look at next." We celebrate the person's progress, not our software.

**Family resemblance rule.** Any two assistants' copy, placed side by side, should feel written by the same company — same warmth, same honesty, same rhythm — while a regular reader could still tell Rocco from Lucy by personality. Shared soul, distinct voice.

## 4. Visual Language

The shared visual DNA of EMG. Each product inherits all of it; only the signature object, the companion character, and domain accents change.

**Typography.** One confident sans-serif family across the ecosystem, used with dramatic scale contrast: very large, tight, bold display headings (letter-spacing slightly negative) against calm, generous, readable body. Few weights, used decisively (regular for body, semibold/bold for emphasis and display). Generous line-height on body (~1.55). Type is a primary design element — we trust big type and whitespace instead of decoration.

**Spacing.** A consistent spacing scale (4px base; steps like 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128). Whitespace is luxury — sections breathe with large vertical rhythm (clamped, responsive). Crowding is the enemy; when unsure, add space.

**Grid.** A simple, centered max-width content column (~1080px) with comfortable gutters. Cards flow in responsive auto-fit grids. Mobile-first: every layout is designed for the phone first, then allowed to expand — never a desktop layout crammed down.

**Motion.** "Alive but calm." One house easing curve (gentle ease-out, ~cubic-bezier(0.2,0.7,0.2,1)). Micro 150-250ms, standard 250-400ms, reveals 600-900ms, ambient loops 4-8s. Everything breathes; nothing performs. Always reduced-motion safe. (Full philosophy in Section 5's sibling, the site bibles.)

**Photography.** Real human moments, warm golden-hour or warm-interior light, documentary primes (35/50/85mm), soft depth, generous negative space, authentic diversity. Never generic stock, sales reps, or fear imagery. Every photo feels like it belongs to the same warm evening.

**Illustration.** Warm, semi-stylized, soft-shape, painterly-light illustration — the signature objects and companions live here. Same lighting world as the photography. Never flat corporate vector clip-art, never cartoonish gimmick.

**Lighting.** The single most unifying choice: **warm golden hour, just after sunset.** Across photos, illustrations, and the signature visualizations, light is low, warm, and reassuring, with soft shadows and glowing warm interiors. The whole ecosystem feels like one safe evening.

**Depth.** Soft, layered, gentle. Achieved with light and shadow, not heavy borders. Subtle parallax and elevation imply space. Flat where calm is needed, dimensional where curiosity is invited (the visualizations).

**Color.** Inherited from the EMG core: **Navy** (trust, structure), **Orange** (help, action, the human/warm layer), **Cream** (calm, clarity), with **Teal/green reserved strictly for system/"alive" status — never homeowner/user decoration.** Each product may introduce one restrained domain accent, but navy + orange + cream + the orange/teal law are constant. This color law is sacred (ADR-009) and ecosystem-wide.

**Cards.** Soft, generous, low-border. Large radius, warm background, layered soft shadow, a quiet hover lift with a gentle glow. Cards are calm containers, not boxed-in compartments — fewer borders, more space.

**Forms.** Conversational and minimal. As few fields as possible, large comfortable targets, gentle focus states (warm glow ring), plain-language labels and helper text. The flagship input is the **Ask bar** — a single, inviting, rounded conversation starter with the companion present inside it. Forms never feel like paperwork.

**Buttons.** Pill-shaped, confident, one clear primary (warm orange) per view. Primary = action/help (orange); secondary = calm (white/navy outline). Hover lifts a few pixels with a soft shadow; never flashes or shouts. One obvious next step beats five options.

**Icons.** Minimal line icons, ~1.6-2px stroke, rounded caps, navy by default with orange for active/help. Consistent geometric family. Icons clarify; they never decorate for decoration's sake.

**Shadows.** Soft, warm-tinted, multi-layered, directional (light from above). Long and diffuse for elevation, never hard or pure-black. Shadows imply gentle depth and calm, not drama.

**Radius.** Generously rounded and consistent: pills (999px) for actions and chips, ~20-28px for cards and the visualization stage, ~12px for small tiles. Roundness signals friendliness and safety.

**Animation.** Reserved for meaning: reveal-on-scroll arrivals, companion idle life, signature-object ambient motion, hover acknowledgments. Never autoplay spectacle. Animation makes the product feel present and alive, not busy.

**Micro-interactions.** The subconscious quality layer: consistent hover easing, focus rings, the companion's blink and gaze, a chip's gentle rise, a window warming on hover. Tiny, consistent, everywhere — this is what makes EMG feel expensive.

**Accessibility.** Non-negotiable and ecosystem-wide. WCAG AA contrast minimum, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic structure and ARIA, respect for `prefers-reduced-motion` (all motion off), readable base sizes, and touch targets >=44px. Calm and inclusive are the same goal: an experience that works for everyone, gracefully.

## 5. The Interactive Visualization Framework

**This is the most important section.** The interactive visualization is EMG's defining product pattern — the signature experience that makes every property instantly recognizable as ours. Where ChatGPT has a blank box, **every EMG product opens with a beautiful, explorable thing you can poke, and every poke becomes a conversation.**

**The pattern.** Each product has one signature, ownable, illustrated visualization at its heart. It is rendered in the shared warm golden-hour world. It is divided into named, glowing hotspots. Hovering or tapping a hotspot lights it up and opens the companion *already knowing* what the person is asking about. The visualization turns an intimidating domain into a calm, guided exploration — and it doubles as the favicon/app-icon DNA of that product.

**The visualization per property:**
- **ServicesInMyCity -> the interactive Home.** Rooms and systems (roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, kitchen, etc.) glow and open Rocco with context. (Canonical reference implementation.)
- **CareInMyCity -> the interactive Human Body.** Gentle, respectful, non-clinical body map; tap an area to ask about symptoms or care, opens the care companion. Warm, never scary or graphic.
- **PetsInMyCity -> the interactive Dog and Cat.** Lovable illustrated pets; tap ears, paws, teeth, belly; opens the pet companion with context. Endearing, not veterinary-cold.
- **SchoolsInMyCity -> the interactive School Campus.** Buildings and spaces (classroom, cafeteria, field, bus loop, library); tap to ask about academics, safety, enrollment.
- **HomesInMyCity -> the interactive Home-Buying Journey.** A path/map of the buying journey (budget, search, offer, inspection, closing); tap a stage to ask what it means and what to do.
- **MarriageInMyCity -> the interactive Relationship Journey.** A warm, respectful path of milestones and moments; tap to explore guidance. Gentle and human above all.
- **GameDayInMyCity -> the interactive Stadium / Field.** A playful stadium or playing field; tap sections (tickets, tailgate, seats, the field) to ask about plans, rules, logistics.

**Every visualization must:**
- **invite exploration** — it looks like something you want to touch.
- **reduce complexity** — it makes a scary domain feel surveyable at a glance.
- **create curiosity** — hotspots and gentle motion reward poking.
- **naturally lead into AI conversation** — every interaction is a doorway to the companion, pre-loaded with context (no blank-box cold start).
- **become instantly recognizable** — one object, one angle, one mood, ownable forever, recognizable from a favicon to full screen.

**Shared construction rules.** Three-quarter, slightly elevated viewing angle; warm dusk lighting; glowing interiors/warm accents; soft ambient motion (breathing, drift, glow) that is subtle and reduced-motion safe; named hotspots with a documented coordinate map; consistent orange "explore/help" hotspot color; companion presence tied to every hotspot. Built in layers for animation, scaling from 32px to full-bleed.

**Why it wins.** It reframes "I have a scary problem / hard decision" into "I have a friendly thing I can explore and ask about." Competitors can copy a graphic; they cannot copy the emotional reframing or the family of worlds it lives in. This single pattern, repeated with a different beautiful object per product, is how a stranger knows three different EMG sites were built by the same company.

## 6. AI Companion Framework

Every EMG product has a named companion. They are a **family of distinct characters living in one shared world** — never recolors of each other, never generic avatars.

**Shared characteristics (the family resemblance):**
- **Warm** — golden-hour lighting, soft features, genuinely friendly.
- **Approachable** — eye-level with the person, never above or beneath them.
- **Trustworthy** — competent and honest; works for the user, not for vendors.
- **Calm** — steady presence, gentle idle motion, never frantic.
- **Visually related** — same lighting world, same illustration language, same warmth, a consistent identity device (e.g. Rocco's name patch), and the orange-equals-help rule.

**The roster.**
- **Rocco (ServicesInMyCity)** — the trusted tradesperson neighbor; navy work shirt, name patch, cap. Warm and steady. The canonical companion.
- **Carl** — a future EMG guide; own face, wardrobe, and domain, same warmth and world.
- **Lucy** — a future EMG guide; her own personality and look, unmistakably family.
- **Coach (GameDayInMyCity feel)** — a touch more energetic, still calm and kind.
- **Future assistants** — each new domain gets its own character, born from this framework.

**The five modes (every companion ships all five):** illustration (canonical branded portrait), photography (warm documentary portraits where real humans fit), icon (a minimal navy glyph + orange spark, legible at 24px), avatar (circular head-and-shoulders with the teal "live" status dot for chat), and hero (full warm presence, idle life, the page's emotional anchor).

**Presence before conversation.** A companion must feel *here* before anyone types: idle breathing/float, slow blink, gaze that drifts toward what the user is doing, a "listening" lean on input focus, a time-of-day greeting, a "thinking" shimmer. The teal dot signals presence; orange signals readiness to help. This is the "someone is already here for me" feeling, ecosystem-wide.

**Unique personality without breaking family.** Each companion has a distinct voice, humor dial, and look — but identical values (warm, honest, on your side), identical world (golden hour), and identical respect for the user. Side by side, a stranger feels "same family, different guide," instantly, without being told.

## 7. Emotional Journey

Every EMG experience moves the person through one intentional emotional progression. Pages are designed to advance this arc, not just to display content.

```
Arrival  ->  Curiosity  ->  Relief  ->  Understanding  ->  Confidence  ->  Action  ->  Trust  ->  Loyalty
```

- **Arrival.** First impression of calm and warmth. Nothing shouts. The person exhales. ("This is different. This feels safe.")
- **Curiosity.** The signature visualization invites a poke. ("Wait — I can just explore and ask about any of this?")
- **Relief.** The companion greets them and meets them where they are. ("Someone competent is already here, on my side.")
- **Understanding.** Plain-language guidance makes the complicated legible. ("Oh — now I get what's actually going on.")
- **Confidence.** They feel equipped, not dependent. ("I know what to ask and what to expect.")
- **Action.** One obvious, low-pressure next step. ("I'll just ask this one question / take this one step.")
- **Trust.** Honesty (including limits and costs) proves we're on their side. ("They told me the truth, even when it didn't sell me anything.")
- **Loyalty.** The experience is calm, useful, and a little delightful, so they come back and bring others. ("I go here first now. I tell my friends.")

**Design rule.** For any new page or section, name where it sits in the arc and the single emotion it must produce, then design toward that emotion and remove anything that dilutes it. Layout serves feeling, always.

## 8. Product Principles

**Every EMG product should feel like software, not a website.** A living, intelligent product you return to — not a page you bounce off.

**It must never feel like:**
- a directory or listings page
- a lead-generation funnel
- a blog or content farm
- a contractor/business template
- an SEO farm built for crawlers, not people

**It must always feel like:**
- an **operating system** — coherent, capable, consistent, a place where things get done.
- an **intelligent assistant** — present, contextual, conversational, helpful before it's asked.
- a **premium consumer product** — crafted details, calm confidence, nothing cheap or noisy.
- **something people enjoy returning to** — alive, a little delightful, worth a visit even without a problem.

**Operating principles:**
- **Conversation over forms.** The Ask bar and companion are the primary interface; paperwork is the exception.
- **Show, don't tell.** Visualize and demonstrate; replace paragraphs with explorable things.
- **One obvious next step.** Reduce choices to the single best move; never overwhelm.
- **Context is carried.** The product remembers what the person was exploring; no cold starts, no repeating yourself.
- **Calm by default.** No popups, no manufactured urgency, no dark patterns, ever.
- **Built to be inherited.** Every pattern is designed to be reused across the ecosystem, not bespoke to one site.
- **Privacy and respect.** We handle people's lives and homes; we are careful, transparent, and never exploit data or attention.

## 9. The Long-Term Vision

**Five years out.** EMG is no longer a set of websites. It is **EMG OS — the trusted AI layer people turn to before they act on the biggest and smallest decisions of their lives.** When something breaks at home, a symptom worries you, a pet seems off, a school decision looms, a house is for sale, a relationship needs care, or game day approaches — you open an EMG property and a familiar, warm guide is already there, in a world you instantly recognize, ready to help you understand before you spend a dollar or make a call.

**What the ecosystem becomes.** Each product is a room in the same house. Rocco, Carl, Lucy, Coach, and their successors are a family of guides who share a soul. A person who trusts Rocco with their home trusts CareInMyCity with a health question and PetsInMyCity with their dog, because it is visibly, unmistakably the same company that earned their trust the first time. One account, one calm world, one standard of honesty — many domains. The interactive visualization is the front door of each; the companion is the friend inside; the warm golden-hour world is the shared address.

**How everything connects.** Shared design DNA, shared companion framework, shared visualization pattern, shared writing voice, shared color law, shared emotional arc, and shared honesty covenant — all defined here, all inherited. Trust compounds across the ecosystem: every honest interaction in one product makes the next product more credible. The brand becomes a promise: *if it's EMG, it's calm, it's honest, and it's on your side.*

**Why this is fundamentally different.**
- **vs. Angi / Thumbtack / Houzz:** they are lead-generation marketplaces that sell your information and pressure a transaction. We are a trusted guide that equips you *before* any transaction and is paid for being genuinely useful, not for selling leads. They optimize for the vendor; we optimize for the person.
- **vs. Google:** Google hands you ten blue links and a wall of ads, then leaves you to sort truth from noise alone. We hand you a calm companion who already understands the domain and your context, and turns searching into a guided conversation.
- **vs. ChatGPT:** a brilliant blank box that assumes you know what to ask. We replace the blank box with a beautiful, explorable world and a domain expert who greets you, knows the context, and is grounded in honesty and real local help. Personality, presence, and a recognizable world — not a generic prompt.

In short: everyone else optimizes for clicks, leads, or queries. **EMG optimizes for trust** — and builds one recognizable, beloved family of AI guides that people return to for life. That is how EMG becomes one of the most recognizable AI consumer brands in the world.
